Most popular camping spots last month

  • SCENIC MOUNTAIN RV PARK RESORT

    Located near Milledgeville and Lake Sinclair, this pet-friendly RV park offers spacious sites for big rigs, license-free fishing ponds, and extensive walking trails. Guests can unwind in the saltwater pool or connect at the clubhouse. Open year-round, it’s an ideal retreat for a peaceful or adventurous escape. With its blend of natural beauty and modern amenities, Scenic Mountain RV Park Resort is the perfect place to create lasting memories.

  • LAKE OCONEE

    Nestled on the southern banks of Lake Oconee, the KOA campground is a haven for those seeking both adventure and tranquility. With boat access, unique railroad boxcar accommodations, and a serene private beach, it offers a picturesque lakeside escape. The clubhouse complements the natural beauty, providing a cozy retreat. Guests can indulge in the best bass fishing, explore forested trails, or simply relax by the water, making every moment at this campground a treasured one.

    Read more  Make a Reservation

  • MISTLETOE STATE PARK

    Mistletoe State Park, on Clarks Hill Lake near Augusta, offers campsites, cottages, and a variety of outdoor activities. Enjoy swimming, boating, canoeing, and trails leading to an ancient rock wall. It’s an ideal spot for fishing, wildlife viewing, and connecting with nature. Whether you’re exploring the trails or relaxing by the lakeshore, Mistletoe State Park promises an unforgettable experience in Georgia’s great outdoors.

    Read more  Make a Reservation

  • FORT MOUNTAIN STATE PARK

    Fort Mountain State Park, spanning 3,712 acres near Chatsworth and Ellijay, boasts an ancient 885-foot rock wall. This mysterious structure, believed to be of Native American origin, adds intrigue to the park. With over 60 miles of scenic trails winding through forests and blueberry thickets, it’s a haven for hikers and nature lovers. Don’t miss the stone fire tower and lakeside beach for memorable moments in Georgia’s serene wilderness. The park also offers rustic cabins for those seeking an overnight escape. Imagine waking up surrounded by nature, with the ancient rock wall as your backdrop. Whether you’re hiking the trails or simply unwinding by the lakeshore, Fort Mountain invites you to create lasting memories in Georgia’s wilderness.

    Read more  Make a Reservation

  • HIGH FALLS STATE PARK

    High Falls State Park, cradled in the embrace of the Towaliga River, spans a majestic 1,050 acres in Jackson, Georgia. Its crowning glory is High Falls, a cascading waterfall that creates a serene and picturesque environment. Visitors can meander along trails that offer tranquil views or engage with the river’s flow on kayaks and canoes. The park’s verdant landscape is home to an array of wildlife, while its historic past as a thriving industrial town adds a layer of intrigue for history buffs. Whether it’s picnicking by the water’s edge or hiking to vantage points that afford panoramic views of the falls and river, High Falls State Park invites you to partake in its blend of natural beauty and historical charm.

    Read more  Make a Reservation

  • VOGEL STATE PARK

    Vogel State Park, cradled at the foot of Blood Mountain in Blairsville, Georgia, covers 233 acres of pristine Appalachian beauty. The park’s centerpiece, Lake Trahlyta, offers a tranquil spot for paddleboats and fishing, with the reflection of the surrounding mountains shimmering on its surface. The park’s cottages and campsites provide a cozy haven for visitors to stay and soak in the panoramic views. Whether trekking along the diverse trails or enjoying a peaceful picnic by the water, Vogel State Park beckons you to indulge in the tranquility of Georgia’s enchanting mountain landscape.

    Read more  Make a Reservation

  • TALLULAH GORGE STATE PARK

    Tallulah Gorge State Park, located at the base of Blood Mountain in the Chattahoochee National Forest, spans 2,689 acres and is one of Georgia’s most captivating natural wonders. The park surrounds Tallulah Gorge, a 1,000-foot-deep chasm carved by the Tallulah River. Visitors can hike rim trails to several overlooks, or they can obtain a permit to hike to the gorge floor (limited to 100 per day, not available during water releases). The park’s rugged terrain, lush forests, and cascading waterfalls provide habitat for unique species. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a nature enthusiast, Tallulah Gorge State Park invites you to immerse yourself in awe-inspiring beauty and daring adventures while connecting with the past in this geologic wonderland.

  • CLOUDLAND CANYON STATE PARK

    Cloudland Canyon State Park, nestled within the rugged beauty of Lookout Mountain, spans 3,485 acres in Rising Fawn, Georgia. Its most captivating feature is Cloudland Canyon, a dramatic gorge carved by Sitton Gulch Creek. Visitors can explore rim trails with breathtaking vistas or venture down to the canyon floor for an up-close encounter with waterfalls. The park’s dense forests harbor diverse wildlife, and the rugged terrain invites hikers and adventure seekers. Whether you’re camping under the stars or marveling at the canyon’s grandeur, Cloudland Canyon State Park beckons you to immerse yourself in Georgia’s natural wonder.

    Read more  Make a Reservation

  • AMICOLOLA FALLS STATE PARK STATE

    Amicalola Falls State Park & Lodge, nestled amidst the natural beauty of North Georgia, spans 829 acres of pristine wilderness. Its most treasured gem is Amicalola Falls, a majestic 729-foot waterfall—the highest in the state. The lodge itself offers awe-inspiring vistas, thrilling zip-lining adventures, expertly guided hikes, and snug accommodations. Whether you’re exploring the network of scenic trails or simply unwinding by the tranquil lakeshore, Amicalola Falls State Park & Lodge invites you to forge enduring memories in Georgia’s serene wilderness.
